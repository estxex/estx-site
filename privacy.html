2.Personal Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal data:

Account data, including your name, email address, phone number, date of birth, nationality, residential address, and account credentials.

Identity verification (KYC) data, including government-issued identification documents, photographs, selfies or liveness checks, proof of address, and information about the source of your funds where required.

Financial and transaction data, including deposit and withdrawal history, trading activity, order history, wallet addresses, linked payment details, and account balances.

Technical data, including IP address, device identifiers, browser type and version, operating system, time zone, and log data generated by your use of the Platform.

Usage data, including pages visited, features used, session duration, and interaction patterns on the Platform.

Communication data, including messages you send to our support channels, survey responses, and marketing preferences.

We collect this data directly from you, automatically through your use of the Platform, and from third-party verification and compliance providers.

3.How We Use Your Personal Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

To create, maintain, and secure your account.

To provide trading, wallet, deposit, and withdrawal services.

To verify your identity and comply with anti-money laundering (AML), counter-terrorist financing (CTF), sanctions, and know-your-customer (KYC) obligations.

To monitor transactions for fraud, market abuse, and suspicious activity.

To comply with legal and regulatory obligations, including reporting to competent authorities where required.

To communicate with you about your account, security events, service changes, and support requests.

To send you marketing communications where you have consented, with the ability to opt out at any time.

To analyze and improve the performance, safety, and usability of the Platform.

To establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

4.Legal Bases for Processing

Where the GDPR applies, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

Performance of a contract, where processing is necessary to provide the services you have requested.

Compliance with legal obligations, including AML/CTF, sanctions, tax, and regulatory requirements.

Legitimate interests, including securing the Platform, preventing fraud, improving our services, and managing our business, provided these interests are not overridden by your rights.

Consent, for optional processing such as marketing communications, which you may withdraw at any time.

5.Sharing of Personal Data

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your personal data with:

Service providers acting on our behalf, including identity verification, cloud hosting, analytics, communications, and customer support providers.

Liquidity providers, banking partners, and payment service providers, to the extent necessary to execute deposits, withdrawals, and trades.

Regulators, supervisory authorities, law enforcement, and other public bodies, where we are legally required or permitted to do so.

Professional advisers, including auditors, accountants, and legal counsel, under obligations of confidentiality.

Corporate transaction parties, in connection with a merger, acquisition, restructuring, or sale of assets, subject to appropriate safeguards.

All third parties processing personal data on our behalf are bound by contractual obligations to protect your data and use it only for the purposes we specify.

6.International Data Transfers

Your personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside Liechtenstein and the European Economic Area, including jurisdictions where our service providers or group entities operate.

Where such transfers occur, we implement appropriate safeguards, such as adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, or other lawful transfer mechanisms, to ensure your data remains protected.

7.Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy.

In particular, identity verification and transaction records are retained for the period required by applicable AML/CTF legislation, which is generally at least five (5) years after the end of the business relationship or the date of the relevant transaction.

Where data is no longer required, it is securely deleted or anonymized.

8.Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data, including encryption in transit, access controls, network security measures, segregation of duties, logging, and regular security reviews.

No system is completely secure. You are responsible for keeping your account credentials and two-factor authentication devices safe and for notifying us immediately of any suspected unauthorized access to your account.

9.Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

The right to access the personal data we hold about you.

The right to rectification of inaccurate or incomplete data.

The right to erasure of your data, where no legal retention obligation applies.

The right to restriction of processing in certain circumstances.

The right to data portability for data you have provided to us.

The right to object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling.

The right to withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

The right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, in particular the Liechtenstein Data Protection Authority (Datenschutzstelle).

Please note that certain rights may be limited where we are subject to legal retention or compliance obligations, including AML/CTF requirements.
